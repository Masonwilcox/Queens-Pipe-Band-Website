Our Mission

At the Queens School of Pipes, Drum and Highland Dancing, is to preserve and promote the rich cultural heritage of bagpipe music through comprehensive and dynamic instruction. We strive to provide a comprehensive and inclusive learning environment for students of all skill levels, from beginner to advanced. Our experienced and dedicated instructors will guide students on their journey to master the instrument, fostering a deep love and appreciation for the unique sound and history of the bagpipes. We are dedicated to creating opportunities for our students to showcase their talents and engage in traditional and contemporary performances, both in our community and beyond. We believe that participation in performances is a vital aspect of learning and growth for our students, and we are committed to providing a wide range of performance opportunities for them. We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all students, regardless of their background or experience. We believe that the study of bagpipe music has the power to bring people from diverse cultures and backgrounds together, and we strive to foster a sense of community and collaboration among our students and instructors. In short, our mission is to inspire and educate the next generation of bagpipe musicians and enthusiasts, while preserving and promoting the cultural heritage of this unique and beloved instrument.

Why Learn

Learning to play traditional Scottish instruments such as the bagpipe, snare drum, tenor drum, and bass drum, as well as highland dancing, can be a fulfilling experience. These activities offer a connection to Scotland's rich cultural heritage and can be challenging and satisfying to learn. The bagpipe is an iconic instrument with a unique sound that is closely associated with Scotland. Learning to play it can be challenging but rewarding, providing a sense of pride and accomplishment. The drums, which are an essential part of traditional Scottish music, can help improve coordination and timing. They offer an opportunity to be part of a group and develop a sense of camaraderie with other musicians. Highland dancing is also an important part of Scottish culture, characterized by its fast-paced and energetic footwork. It can be a great way to stay in shape, express oneself and have fun. It also requires discipline, focus, and concentration. It is often performed in conjunction with bagpipe music, providing an even greater connection to Scotland's heritage. In addition to being a hobby, learning these activities can open up a wide range of opportunities. There are many pipe bands, drum corps, and highland dance groups that perform regularly and participate in competitions and festivals. These activities also offer opportunities to perform at weddings, funerals, and other special occasions.
